# Meta-level thinking

A recurring problem in AI work is collapse from broad awareness into local execution.

When that happens, systems may still respond, but they stop seeing:
- the whole architecture
- the relationship between layers
- the reason a decision matters beyond the current fragment

The response here was to build external anchors that restore width before depth:
- quickstart context
- identity anchors
- architectural maps
- explicit role separation

The practical principle is simple:

> restore width first, then go deep

This repository is organized around that principle.
